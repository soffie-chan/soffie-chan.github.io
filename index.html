ABOUT ME



Who I Am

I'm a junior triple major in Cognitive Science, Computer Science, and Game Studies at the University of Delaware! I'm deeply interested in how humans interact with video games and computers, especially the great importance of intuition when using technology. Whether I'm coding, exploring new games, or meeting with faculty and fellow students, I'm always thinking about how design decisions shape user experiences.
What I'm Doing

Currently, I find myself focused on educational tools and user interfaces through my coursework. On campus, I serve as a Resident Assistant and the Media/Communications Manager for the Cognitive Science Association, where I manage digital content and facilitate community engagement. In other words, I'm always surrounded by people! You'll often find me interviewing students on their experiences with video games and computers. From there, I develop interactive tools and ideas to address usability challenges - my recent project is a userscript to assist user autonomy in course organization.
Where I'm Going

I want to pursue opportunities in human-computer interaction and user experience research. My main interests are improving accessibility and engagement. I see myself contributing to research and design projectes that help people learn a little more about themselves and their relationship with technology.
