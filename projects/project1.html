OVERVIEW
As part of the Computer Education Technology course, our team of two developed Canvas Shortcuts - an extension that allows users to create a shortcut to a specific part of any course. This way, users can directly navigate to the page they wish without going through multiple steps, which was a self-reported problem many users were facing. We also developed tools within the overlay to customize each Shortcut to the user's liking.


ROLE
I interviewed various students, created the shortcut edit and delete modes, utilized Web Storage API to retain shortcuts, and created a functioning filing system.

THE PROBLEM

Canvas is the chosen educational management tool used by the University of Delaware, where I am a student. However, many professors use Canvas differently - some of them don't realize that they hide their most valuable resources into hard-to-access spaces, whereas some clutter their Canvas page and enhance cognitive overload for students. About 40% of the students interviewed stated that they were often stressed by having to change their method of navigation for each course.

THE METHODS AND PROCESS

I interviewed various students across different years through verbal conversation. I asked a series of questions, including how often they use Canvas, which courses they used Canvas for, and what their most prominent pain points were. 40% of students said the same pain point - professors arranging their Canvas page in different ways makes it that much more harder for them to easily navigate through the page, as they have to keep adapting to each course page.


Furthermore, we built off of Dr. Austin Cory Bart's "More Canvas Tools" extension by obtaining the userscript and crafting one of our own using Typescript, CSS, HTML, and TamperMonkey. We coded an overlay by first enabled creation and addition of shortcuts to a list. Then, we allowed users to edit and delete their shortcuts. Lastly, we created a filing system, such that users could also organize their shortcuts based on course name, due date, and more. The files were also made to be editable and deletable.
