OVERVIEW
As part of the Introduction to Computer Science course at UD, our team of two developed Cherrim Bloom! Inspired by Lights Out, players are expected to click in patterns to make the blossom Pokemon, Cherrim, achieve a happy, blooming state. Each click allows users to see what pattern is being clicked, and a customizable overlay enables players to customize the grid and the patterns used. Furthermore, steps can be solved if players are stuck.


ROLE
I created our basic prototype of the game. I made the patterns of clicks, the grid, the click counter, and some interactive features.

THE METHODS AND PROCESS

We were provided with the WebEz Library as part of our course material. After rigorous practice and reviewing documentation, our final project paired me with Sophia Zhang - we instantly connected over our shared love for Pokemon. From that point, we decided to add a fun twist to our Lights Out game.


We wanted to make sure that our game implemented the main concept of Lights Out - random squares would represent "on", and the game would involve attempts to turn the lights off. The catch is that your cursor clicks in various patterns, requiring the user to choose their next move carefully. I started by coding Lights Out as it is - a few colorful boxes, a few dark boxes, and a clicking pattern. Sophia used her artistic skills to replace the colorful boxes with blooming sunshine Cherrims - represented by a bright pink flower. The dark boxes were the overcast Cherrim buds. We picked Cherrim as our Pokemon because of these two forms that acted like a light!


After our game came to life, we went the extra mile to implement "automatic solving" with the click of a button to solve a step if users were stuck. Lastly, we added different kinds of clicking patterns and allowed for grid modification.


INSIGHTS & REFLECTION

As my first game, I really enjoyed having so much creative freedom. Learning to find a creative voice through a static course project meant a lot to us as a team.


What Worked Well? Not only was the project aesthetically pleasing - we did not require an extensive tutorial for the game. It taught me that some of the simplest, fun games can let you grab your console of choice and dive in without having to stress over varying controls.


What Would I Improve? We did not push the WebEz library to its fullest potential. Looking forward, I would like for us to use the different effects and tools that libraries have to offer. Instead of sticking to our idea, it's important to note of what we are capable of through our resources to push our ideas to better extents!
